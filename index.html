The Stewart Memorial Fountain


The Stewart Memorial Fountain is a historic monument located in Kelvingrove Park, Scotland. It was commissioned in memory of Lord Provost Robert Stewart of Murdostoun, who served the city of Glasgow during the mid-19th century. The fountain was designed by the renowned Scottish sculptor James Sellars and was completed in 1872.
Crafted in ornate cast iron and decorated with intricate sculptural details, the fountain reflects the Victorian era’s appreciation for elaborate public monuments. Figures and decorative motifs symbolize civic pride, prosperity, and the importance of public service. Originally painted in bright colors, as was typical for Victorian cast-iron structures, the fountain served not only as a decorative landmark but also as a functional public water source.
Today, the Stewart Memorial Fountain remains an important heritage feature within Kelvingrove Park, representing Glasgow’s rich architectural and cultural history.
